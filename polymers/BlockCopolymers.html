Block Copolymers

A block copolymer is a polymer of the form

(A)x(B)y(C)z ...

An important subset are the diblock copolymers. The results we will derive for them, can be easily applied to more complicated block arrangements.

Typically we will know the chemical composition of each monomer, the repeat numbers of the blocks x=NA, y=NB, ..., and the densities of the related homopolymers rA, rB, ... . From the chemical composition we can immediately determine the molar masses MA, MB, ... and the amount of charge per monomer  ZA, ZB, ...

The parameter that determines the phase of the block copolymer is the ratio of the block volumes. First of all, the molar volume is given by

vA = MA / rA

Then the block volume per mol is

VA = NA vA

Hence the ratio of the block volumes is given by

VA/VB = {NA vA}/{NB vB} = {NA MA rB} / {NB MB rA}

For 0.4 < VA/VB < 0.6, diblock copolymers form a lamellar phase which are symmetric for VA=VB. More asymmetric diblock copolymers form hexagonally packed cylinders or BCC-packed spheres. In small intermediate regions of the phase diagram bicontinuous phases (gyroid, plummer's nightmare) can occur.
